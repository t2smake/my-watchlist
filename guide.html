How to Read the Watchlist

Signals, tags, views, and filters — explained.

01 Overview

The watchlist tracks 57 tickers across 19 sectors, updated once per trading day. Each ticker is evaluated using RSI(14) and key moving averages (MA50, MA200) to surface long-term entry awareness signals — not trade recommendations.

Data is fetched from Yahoo Finance's public API and cached in your browser's localStorage. Repeat opens are instant. The market context bar at the top of the page shows SPY, QQQ, IWM, and DIA as broad market benchmarks.

02 Signal Definitions

Each ticker card displays one signal. Signals are assigned in priority order — the first matching rule wins.

⬇ DEEP DISCOUNT
RSI < 32
Heavily oversold. Historically a potential accumulation zone — price has shed significant momentum and may be approaching a reversal area. Highest priority signal.
◎ WATCH ENTRY
RSI 32–45 · within 8% of MA200
Recovering from oversold while sitting near long-term support. Both conditions must be true: RSI in the recovery range and price close to the 200-day moving average.
↓ NEAR SUPPORT
Price within 3% below MA50 or MA200
Approaching a key moving average from below. Often a decision point — price may bounce off support or break through it. Watch for volume confirmation.
↑ UPTREND
Price above MA50 & MA200
Confirmed uptrend — price is holding above both the 50-day and 200-day moving averages. No immediate entry urgency; useful for monitoring trend health.
⚠ EXTENDED
RSI > 72 or far above MA200
Overbought or significantly extended above long-term average. Elevated entry risk. Not a short signal — simply a caution flag for new positions.
03 Tag System

Small inline tags appear on each card and sector header, giving a quick read on momentum and structure.

RSI 38 RSI 55 RSI 74
RSI tag — 14-period Relative Strength Index. Green when below 40 (oversold territory), red when above 70 (overbought), amber in between.
↑50 ↓50
MA50 tag — Arrow shows whether price is currently above (↑, green) or below (↓, red) the 50-day simple moving average.
↑200 ↓200
MA200 tag — Same as MA50 but for the 200-day SMA. Indicates long-term trend alignment. Most significant MA level for position traders.
↑ GOLDEN ↓ DEATH
Cross tag — GOLDEN means MA50 is above MA200 (bullish trend structure). DEATH means MA50 has crossed below MA200 (bearish trend structure). Shows broad trend direction for the ticker.
1.4× 0.8×
Volume tag — Today's volume as a multiple of the 20-day average. Values above 1.2× indicate elevated participation; below 0.8× is quiet. Green when above average, muted otherwise.
04 Sector Headers & ETF Benchmarks

Each sector group header shows the closest benchmark ETF for that theme — for example, GDX for Gold Miners, TAN for Solar, XLF for Financials.

What the header shows
The ETF symbol, today's price change (e.g. +0.8%), and the same RSI / MA / Cross tags used on individual ticker cards — applied to the ETF itself. This gives you a quick read on the sector's own momentum before drilling into individual names.
Why it matters
When the sector ETF is showing a NEAR SUPPORT or DEEP DISCOUNT signal at the same time as individual tickers — that's sector-level confluence. Tickers in a healthy-ETF sector with UPTREND signals are in a different risk context than the same signal in a sector with a DEATH cross.
05 Market Bar

The four tiles at the top of the page — SPY, QQQ, IWM, DIA — provide broad market context. Each tile shows:

Price & daily change — green for up, red for down.

52-week range bar — a gold dot on a gradient track showing where current price sits within its annual range. Far left is near the 52W low; far right is near the 52W high.

RSI / MA / Cross tags — same tag system as individual tickers, applied to the index ETF.

Use the market bar to judge overall market health. Entering individual positions while SPY and QQQ are showing EXTENDED signals and DEATH crosses is meaningfully different from entering when they're at support with bullish structure.

07 Filters
Signal filter
Buttons below the legend — click any signal name to show only tickers matching that signal. Useful for quickly finding all DEEP DISCOUNT or WATCH ENTRY names across all sectors. Click again to clear.
Sector tabs
Tabs at the top scroll left/right across all 19 sectors. Selecting a sector shows only that group. Activating a sector tab clears the signal filter, and vice versa — only one filter is active at a time.
08 52-Week Position

Each card shows where the current price sits within its 52-week high/low range. The range is divided into three buckets:

NEAR LOW
Bottom 25% of the 52W range. Potential value zone — price is near its annual floor.
MID RANGE
Middle 25–75% of the 52W range. Neutral position relative to recent history.
NEAR HIGH
Top 25% of the 52W range. Extended relative to recent history — consider alongside signal.
09 Data & Cache
Source
Yahoo Finance public API. End-of-day data only — intraday prices are not available through this source.
Cache behavior
Data is fetched once per calendar day and stored in browser localStorage. Subsequent opens within the same day use the cached copy — no network requests, instant load. The status bar shows the data date and whether it came from cache or a live fetch.
Force refresh
To clear the cache and fetch fresh data, open your browser's developer console and run:

localStorage.clear()

Then reload the page. All data will be fetched again from Yahoo Finance.
10 CSV Export

Click the ⬇ EXPORT CSV button in the controls bar to download all currently visible tickers as a comma-separated file. If a filter is active, only the filtered tickers are exported. Columns include symbol, name, price, daily change, RSI, MA50, MA200, signal, and 52W position.
